Face-recognition software be perfect – 
if you’re a white man 

By Timothy Revell 

Algorithms that govern face recognition be bias 

Algorithms that govern face recognition be bias 

CHANDAN KHANNA/AFP/Getty Images 

Face-recognition software can guess your gender with amaze 

accuracy – if you be a white man. 

Joy Buolamwini at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology test 

three commercially available face-recognition systems, create by 

Microsoft, IBM and the Chinese company Megvii. 

The system correctly identify the gender of white men 99 per cent of 

the time. But the error rate rise for people with darker skin, reach 

nearly 35 per cent for women. The result will be present at the 

Conference on Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency in New York 

late this month

Face-recognition software be already be use in many different 

situations, include by police to identify suspect in a crowd and to 

automatically tag photos. 

This mean inaccuracy could have consequences, such a 

systematically ingrain bias in police stop and searches. 

Biases in artificial intelligence system tend to come from bias in the 

data they be train on. According to one study, a widely use data set 

be around 75 per cent male and more than 80 per cent white.
