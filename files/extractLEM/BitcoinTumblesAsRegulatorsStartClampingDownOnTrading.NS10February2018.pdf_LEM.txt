Bitcoin tumble a regulator start 
clamp down on trading 

BITCOIN’S boom and bust continues. Towards the end of last year, the 

digital currency be trading at just under $20,000. This week it dipped 

below $6000. 

Turbulence have come to be expect with cryptocurrencies, and the 

late drop isn’t the big ever. But this week have see a flurry of 

announcement that seem to be contribute to bitcoin’s bursting 

bubble. 

The People’s Bank of China say it will block foreign digital currency 

trading website use the so-called Great Firewall of China. It have 

already shut down domestic exchanges. Regulators in South Korea, 

Russia and India have also announce clampdowns. And some bank 

have ban customer from buying bitcoin with their credit cards, with 

Lloyds Banking Group and Virgin Money both add to that list on 

Monday. 

Many say that the current cryptocurrency bubble have to burst, the only 

question remain is: how low will bitcoin go?
