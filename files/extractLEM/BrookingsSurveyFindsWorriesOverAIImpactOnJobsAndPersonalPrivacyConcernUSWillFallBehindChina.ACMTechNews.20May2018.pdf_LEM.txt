To examine attitude towards AI, researcher at the Brookings Institution undertook an online national survey of 1,535 adult Internet 
user between May 9 and May 11, 2018. It be overseen May 9 to 11, 2018 by Darrell M. West, vice president of Governance Studies and

Privacy 

Only 5 percent say they expect artificial intelligence to increase personal privacy, 12 percent felt it would have no effect on personal 
privacy, 49 percent claim it would reduce personal privacy, and 34 percent didn’t know or give no answer. Males (54 percent) be 
more likely than woman (44 percent) to believe AI would reduce privacy. The same be true for those age 55 to 64 year old. 

Daily life 

When ask how artificial intelligence would affect their day-to-day life, 34 percent say it would make their life easier, 13 percent say 
harder, and 53 percent didn’t know or give no answer. Men (41 percent) be more likely to think AI would make their life easy than 
woman (28 percent), and those under the age of 35 be most likely to feel that way.
